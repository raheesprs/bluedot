When immediate access makes the difference

In critical situations, the challenge is often not just care, but reaching the patient in time.

Helicopter Emergency Medical Services (HEMS) are used when patients need urgent medical attention in locations that are difficult to access by road, or when ground transport would cause delays that could impact outcomes.

This includes remote areas, offshore locations, accident sites and urban environments where speed is critical.

How HEMS support works

HEMS combines rapid deployment with on-site medical care and immediate transport, ensuring patients receive timely intervention and safe transfer.

It begins with rapid activation

Once a request is received, aircraft and medical teams are mobilised immediately, with routing and landing considerations managed in real time.

Then care is delivered on-site

Medical teams assess and stabilise the patient at the scene, providing critical care before transport begins.

Rapid transfer to the right facility

Patients are transported directly to the most appropriate hospital, with continuous medical care maintained throughout the journey.

Who HEMS services are for

HEMS is commonly used for:

  • Emergency response and trauma cases
  • Remote or hard-to-reach locations
  • Offshore and industrial environments
  • Situations where rapid access to care is critical

Choosing the right level of support

HEMS is suitable when immediate response and rapid access are required, particularly in locations where ground transport is not practical or would cause delays.

For longer-distance transfers or cases requiring extended in-flight care, air ambulance or commercial airline medical transfer may be more appropriate. Our team can assess each situation and recommend the safest option.
