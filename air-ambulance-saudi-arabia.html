About Saudi Arabia

The Kingdom of Saudi Arabia (KSA) is the fifth-largest country in Asia, the second-largest in the Arab world, and the largest in Western Asia. It is bordered by the Red Sea to the west; Jordan, Iraq, and Kuwait to the north; the Persian Gulf, Qatar, and the United Arab Emirates to the east; Oman to the southeast; and Yemen to the south. Bahrain is an island country off the east coast of the Kingdom.bIts capital and largest city are Riyadh. The country is also home to Mecca and Medina, the two holiest cities in the Islamic world, which see millions of international pilgrims every year.

Jeddah International Airport

The Kingdom’s second-largest city and biggest seaport are in the city of Jeddah. The city houses the Jeddah International Airport (King Abdulaziz International), the country’s third-largest and busiest airport (serving more than 41 million passengers in 2018). JED sees the highest number of international travelers in the Kingdom and is the largest hub for Saudi Arabia’s flag carrier, Saudia. The airport provides facilities required for the service of pilgrims and those who visit the country to perform Umrah. It has three operational passenger terminals: the North Terminal, the Hajj Terminal, and the new Terminal 1. The Hajj Terminal was specially built for Muslim pilgrims going to Mecca annually on the Hajj.

Tourism Destination

While most tourism in Saudi Arabia largely involves religious pilgrimages, there is growth in the leisure tourism sector due to the opening up of a number of historical and naturally rich destinations around the country. Tourism is an important component of the Saudi Vision 2030, a state-promoted guideline to diversify the economy and develop public service sectors such as health, education, infrastructure, recreation, and tourism. According to research conducted prior to the COVID-19 pandemic, domestic tourist trips were projected to rise 8 percent in 2019, while inbound visits from international markets were expected to grow by 5.6 percent per year. The overall number of tourist trips was projected to hit 93.8 million by 2023, up from 64.7 million in 2018.

Healthcare Services in Saudi Arabia

Saudi hospitals, medical centers, dispensaries, and clinics, include those managed by the Ministry of Public Health, the Ministry of Defence, one university hospital, and one hospital for the Royal Oman Police. The Saudi Ministry of Health has established standards, policies, and funding for the expansion of public healthcare facilities in Oman.

Air Ambulance in Saudi Arabia

Bluedot provides air ambulance services from all major city centres and hospitals in Saudi Arabia. It is our mission to provide a travel experience that is as safe, comfortable, and stress-free as possible for patients and loved ones alike.

The Saudi Red Crescent Authority (SRCA) also runs the Saudi Air Ambulance service, whose fleet includes helicopters and fixed-wing aircraft. The Air Ambulance service has a particular role to play during the Hajj (the annual pilgrimage of Muslims to Mecca), providing on-the-spot first aid to take emergency cases to the nearest medical facilities.

Bluedot Air Ambulance services include medical repatriation flights as well as medical assistance flights. Allied Bluedot services include chartered flights and medical escort services, as well as FAA-approved medical stretchers on commercial flights. Air Ambulance Services are often used for repatriation by embassies and consulates in countries with big expatriate populations.
