About Bahrain

Officially, the Kingdom of Bahrain, Bahrain is situated between Qatar and the northeastern coast of Saudi Arabia, to which it is connected by the famous King Fahd Causeway. The country comprises a small archipelago made up of 50 natural islands and 33 artificial islands, centered on Bahrain Island which makes up around 83 percent of the country’s landmass. The capital and largest city are Manama. It has a total population of 1.5 million and spans 760 square kilometers and is the third-smallest nation in Asia after the Maldives and Singapore.

Bahrain International Airport

Bahrain International Airport (IATA: BAH, ICAO: OBBI) is located on Muharraq Island, adjacent to the capital Manama, and serves as the hub for the national carrier Gulf Air. The airport is managed by the Bahrain Airport Company. BAH is one of the oldest international airports in the Gulf and was established in 1927. Recently it underwent a $1.1 billion expansion, boosting the airport’s capacity to 14 million passengers annually, and 130,000 air traffic movements per year with a handling capacity of 4,700 bags per peak hour. The new terminal at BAH features premium-class check-in halls, check-in desks, passport control booths, E-gates, security lanes, a 9,000 sqm duty-free retail space, premium-class lounges, food and beverage zones, 24 departure gates, and 7,000 new parking spaces goth at-grade and in multi-story facilities.

Health Care Services in Bahrain

All hospitals in Bahrain are subject to inspection and accreditation by the National Health Regulatory Authority (NHRA), an independent regulatory body established in 2010. Bahrain’shospitals, medical centres, pharmacies, physicians, surgeons, nurses, pharmacists, medical technicians and allied healthcare institutions and healthcare professionals must be registered with, and/or licensed, by NHRA. The first hospital in Bahrain was the American Mission Hospital, which opened in 1893 as a dispensary. The first public hospital, and also the tertiary hospital, to open in Bahrain was the Salmaniya Medical Complex, in the Salmaniya district of Manama, in 1957. Private hospitals are also present throughout the country, such as the International Hospital of Bahrain.
